Why track your heart rate?

Your heart is the machine that powers your body.
It is amazingly efficient and powerful, but it will only beat so many times before it gives out.
That is why it is important to do what you can to maintain a good heart rate.

Generally speaking, a lower heart rate means a healthier heart.
And a healthier heart means better quality of life!

Tracking your heart rate will allow you to see your progress or lack thereof over time.
It will then be easier for you to correlate which activities are leading you to a good heart rate.

The more you lower, the more you save

If you can lower your heart rate by 1 Beat Per Minute, you will save nearly 525,600 heart beats in 1 year.
At an average heart rate of 70 Beats Per Minute, that's 5 heart days of life.

Imagine lowering your heart rate by 5 Beats Per minute.
You will save nearly 2,628,000 heart beats in 1 year.
That's 26 heart days of life, almost 1 month!

Think about it.
With every beat you lower, you can save millions of beats over your lifetime.
That's years of heart life; and imagine the increase in your quality of life.
